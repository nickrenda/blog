We will prove the equivalence of the two following definitions of uniform integrability.

Suppose (X, 𝒮, μ) is a measure space and {fλ : λ ∈ Λ} is a family of complex-valued measurable functions on X.

Def 1: The family {fλ : λ ∈ Λ} is said to be uniformly integrable if
limt → ∞supλ∫ |fλ|⋅I(|fλ|>t) dμ = 0.  (1)

Def 2: The family {fλ : λ ∈ Λ} is said to be uniformly integrable if for all ϵ > 0, there exists δ > 0 such that
supλ∫A |fλ| dμ < ϵ  (2)
whenever A ∈ 𝒮 such that μ(A)<δ.
